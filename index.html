I am a Ph.D. student in Pure Mathematics at Memorial University of Newfoundland. My research interests lie broadly in algebra, with particular emphasis on Lie theory, gradings on Lie algebras, algebraic groups, and combinatorial game theory. My recent work includes research on misère games, cyclic group gradings in prime characteristic, and fine gradings on exceptional Lie algebras such as E6.

Alongside my research, I have several years of teaching experience across undergraduate and graduate mathematics. I have taught courses in real analysis, mathematical finance, and advanced group theory, and I have also worked extensively as a teaching assistant, giving tutorials, marking assignments, and supporting students in the Math Help Centre. Beyond mathematics, I have contributed to curriculum development for accessibility-focused education projects and completed a data science project on predicting hospital readmissions using machine learning.
